Balut

Ingredients

  • 1 fertilized duck egg (balut)
  • Water (for boiling)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Vinegar (optional, for dipping)
  • Chili (optional, for dipping)

Instructions

  1. Start by placing the fertilized duck eggs in a saucepan or pot. Add enough water to cover the eggs.
  2. Bring the water to a boil over medium-high heat. Once boiling, lower the heat and let the eggs simmer for about 30 to 40 minutes, depending on how well-cooked you like the yolk and embryo.
  3. Once the eggs are cooked, carefully remove them from the water and let them cool for a few minutes.
  4. Once cool enough to handle, gently tap the egg on the side to crack the shell. Peel off the shell carefully, starting from the pointed end.
  5. Once the shell is removed, you'll see the liquid inside. You can sip the broth or discard it, depending on your preference.
  6. After peeling the shell, you can eat the embryo, the yolk, and the white, all of which are considered delicacies.
  7. Balut is typically served with a pinch of salt, and sometimes vinegar and chili are used as dipping sauces.
  8. Enjoy your authentic Filipino street food!
